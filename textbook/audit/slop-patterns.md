# Slop Patterns

- Avoid generic motivational bridge prose when expanding the slide-derived chapters.
- Watch for service-marketing adjectives such as "seamless", "holistic" and "transformative"; replace them with the actual handoff, control or workflow.
- Avoid "journey" unless it names a specific artefact such as a journey map; prefer request path, workflow, handoff or lifecycle.
- Meta-openers like "this chapter explores" or "it is important to note" should become a concrete workplace problem or decision.
