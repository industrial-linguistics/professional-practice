## Running the weekly ops sync

The weekly sync should feel like a high-signal stand-up, not a status monologue. Encourage learners to cap the session at 30 minutes with a three-slide deck: performance snapshot, escalations, and upcoming changes. Assign owners to every yellow or red metric before the call ends and log due dates in the shared tracker. Anything without a name or deadline will resurface as an incident later.

Use the final minutes for a "no surprises" scan. Ask explicitly about launches, audits, marketing campaigns, or staffing changes that could impact capacity. Offer a concrete prompt: "We're launching the Black Friday campaign next week and expect 10x trafficâ€”can your monitoring handle the alert volume?" That habit gives vendors permission to flag constraints before they become outages and reinforces that the start-up wants partnership, not heroics. It also prevents the 3 a.m. vendor panic call that starts with "We didn't know you were deploying today...".
